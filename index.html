Antonio Reynoso is Running for Re-Election

By: Lynn Barbera

October 5, 2025

What is a Borough President's Job?

A Borough President is responsible for proposing a borough budget to the City Council, prioritizing funds based on the borough’s needs, overseeing decisions about land use and city facilities in the borough, ensuring the communities within their borough have access to city services, and planning for the future of the borough.

Antonio Reynoso has served as Brooklyn Borough President since November 2021, and he is seeking re-election to serve a second term.

Supporting Brooklyn Communities

As Brooklyn Borough President, Reynoso has recognized the diverse needs of the many communities that make up the borough by holding community roundtables and allocating funds to cultural interest groups such as Mixteca and the Arab American Association of New York. He has put money toward community programming through the NYCHA Tenant Association and has expanded financial services in underserved areas like Red Hook.

In his next term, Reynoso plans to continue the work he started by:

  • Launching “Light up the Hood,” a program to fund street lighting throughout East New York, making the area safer and more walkable
  • Improving financial services and lending options in underserved Community District 18
  • Carrying out the “Community Infrastructure Element” of the Comprehensive Plan for Brooklyn, ensuring that community-based organizations have funding and access to government resources

Endorsed by: Flatbush Jewish Community Coalition, Sephardic Community Federation, Crown Heights United, Crown Heights PAC and Muslim Vote Project

Tackling Racial Bias in Maternal Healthcare

Reynoso came into office with improved maternal healthcare on his agenda. He allocated 2023’s capital budget entirely to the cause – renovating NICUs and redesigning labor-and-delivery rooms at Brooklyn public hospitals. He also put money towards resources for neighborhoods with the worst maternal health outcomes, including a public education campaign and postpartum resource distribution.

Reynoso plans to continue:

  • Investing in maternal health infrastructure in more Brooklyn public hospitals
  • Expanding his Community Baby Shower Fund, which provides families in high-risk neighborhoods with resources and support for pregnancy and postpartum care
  • Supporting the Maternal Health Taskforce, a group of eight Black women OBGYNs who Reynoso assembled to work on community-based solutions to the racial disparities in maternal morbidity rates

Endorsed by Planned Parenthood of Greater New York Votes PAC, New York State Nurses Association (NYSNA), and Brooklyn-Queens National Organization of Women

Expanding LGBTQ+ Rights

During his first term as borough president, Reynoso worked to uplift queer and trans voices. He co-sponsored the AFFIRM Act, which protects LGBTQ+ civil rights and healthcare access; secured resources for homeless LGBTQ+ youth through the Ali Forney Center, and spoke out against anti-LGBTQ+ policies.

In his next term, Reynoso plans to:

  • Fully fund programs like the Trans Equity Initiative to support trans and gender nonconforming New Yorkers access education, legal assistance and healthcare navigation
  • Fight against “Multi-Agency Response to Cultural Hotspot” (M.A.R.C.H.) raids, which incite fear and instability for LGBTQ+ businesses
  • Stand up to federal fiscal attacks on LGBTQ+ organizations by allocating the borough president’s budget towards marginalized groups
Endorsed by: The Stonewall Democratic Club of New York City, The Jim Owles Liberal Democratic Club, and The Lambda Independent Democrats of Brooklyn

Providing Affordable, Safe Housing

Reynoso’s “Comprehensive plan for Brooklyn” says it all – the borough president is invested in an equitable future for all Brooklynites. The plan lays out a guide for the borough’s future with an emphasis on creating affordable housing and protecting tenants.

Here’s how Reynoso will enact his plan:

  • Host Tenant’s Rights Forums to educate and empower Brooklyn renters
  • Create a Tangled Title Fund, modeled after Philadelphia’s, which will cover administrative and legal costs for homeowners to prevent deed theft and keep Black and Brown families the owners of their generational homes
  • Reform NYC’s building code to reflect technological advancements in development, leading to a quicker process for creating safe, affordable housing
Endorsed by: Churches United For Fair Housing (CUFFH), the Working Families Party, and New York Communities for Change

Sources: Reynoso Campaign Website , Office of the Brooklyn Borough President , Greenbook, NYC Health + Hospitals
